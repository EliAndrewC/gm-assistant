LORD MOON'S HEAVENLY COURT:

Since the Karmic Inquisitors are PCs who are members of The Order of Lord Moon, let's go over some details about the members of Lord Moon's heavenly court which are worshipped by The Order.

Members of The Order become disciples of one or more servants of Lord Moon - members of his heavenly court. At present, there are three different levels of abilities, referred to as the Crescent Moon Phase of abilities, the Half Moon Phase of abilities, and the Gibbous Moon phase of abilities.

Each PC begins the campaign with three Phases worth of abilities. You may mix and match progression in discipleship of any of Lord Moon's celestial servants, e.g. you can take the Crescent Moon abilities of three different celestials or the Crescent Moon, Half-Moon, and Gibbous Moon abilities of a single servant.

Until recently, the Crescent Moon abilities were all that were known to The Order. Only after The Order helped Lord Moon's servants enter the city of Toshi Ranbo 9 years ago did members begin progressing to higher levels of mastery.

As a reminder of what is also described on the Timeline, it is known that possessing only Crescent Moon abilities makes you safe even if put to the test for being a moon cultist. It is also known the possessing even one Gibbous Moon ability will cause you to fail such a test, showing yourself to be a moon cultist if tested in this way. It is unknown what would occur if someone with Half Moon abilities were tested. Because this is known by all members of The Order, some initiates have made the deliberate decision to limit their progression in the discipleship of any one path, spreading out their mastery to remain safe.

Ryoshun - the Guardian or the Protector:

In the early days of creation, the celestial realms and the mortal realm were closely intertwined, with celestial beings freely passing between them. The heavenly beings of the day and night roamed the world, upsetting the balance of the elements as they strove to each shape the world according to their particular whims and wills. Lord Moon saw the danger this posed to the balance of creation, and tasked his servant Ryoshun with guarding the threshold to the celestial heavens.

Ryoshun is easily identifiable as the star at the highest point in the night sky directly above the world. He watches the skies to prevent other celestials from entering the mortal realm without permission from Lord Moon or Lady Sun, but he is more lenient about allowing denizens from the mortal realm to pass freely across the threshold if they are able to find the way. Such passage is easier when the distances between the realms is at its lowest, as it is now understood to be in the years around a Day of Thunder.

Indeed, it was Ryoshun himself who first entered the Damasu domain in the aftermath of the Tsume invasion. The ancestral god of the Damasu was not being properly worshipped, and so he was receptive to Ryoshun's proposal to allow members of Lord Moon's heavenly court to enter the domain. However, even having been granted this permission, they were still forbidden by Lady Sun to enter the Empire's cities without being granted access by an authority under her purview. Ryoshun gained this by striking a bargain with an Imperial Magistrate from the Yasuki family who had been assigned to oversee the Damasu domain under Tsume rule, and so the moon cult began to spread.

Nanube - the Deceiver or the Liar:

Lord Moon's heavenly court is raucous and keeping his celestial nobles from each other's throats requires care and attention. To this end, Lord Moon assigned his servant Nanube to be his court entertainer and bid her to distract and divert his other servants with entertaining tales and confusing lies. Nanube was unlike any other being in the heavens - quick-witted and clever, she had a silver tongue that could charm even the most skeptical of beings. She could spin a tale like no other, and her lies were so convincing that even the gods themselves were fooled.

Nanube's characteristic approach to influencing Lord Moon's other servants is to spin tales related to their conflicts and weave in subtle references to the circumstances of their present strife. This frequently involves making up stories about the other beings involved, and fabricating details which cast their actions in a different light. Lord Moon looks on approvingly, for he understands that lies can help forge bonds that truth never could.

Disciples of Nanube are able to use deception not merely to conceal their own thoughts, but to influence others and take control of social situations.

Goju - the Hunter or the Tracker:

When the world was new, wild spirits roamed creation sowing chaos. Lady Sun set about assigning names to all of the beings in the celestial heavens and in the mortal realm under heaven. Lady Sun's heavenly court was obedient, and her subjects accepted the names which she assigned. However, Lord Moon's subjects were more rebellious, and many of them refused to be bound by the names which would constrain their shape and nature.

These spirits raced between the realms, as their names trailed behind them, following along the connection created by Lady Sun when she assigned their names. Lord Moon selected his servant Goju to chase down these servants, following their trails and trapping them in place long enough for their names to catch up so they could be bound. It took ages for Goju's work to be complete, in which time he became the greatest hunter and tracker in all of creation. The craftiest of the nameless spirits are still said to race through creation, never staying anywhere long enough to allow their names to catch up with them, and always staying well-hidden to evade the watchful gaze of the heavens.

Disciples of Goju learn to recognize the rhythms and balances of the world, seeing all things in their place and all things out of their place.

Suro - the Thief or the Taker:

In the beginning there was Nothing, and Nothing was lonely. Nothing's desire for companionship created the first third of existence. However, Nothing became afraid of what it had done, and that fear created the second third of existence. Eventually, Nothing came to regret what it had done, and this regret created the final third of existence.

After this occurred, there remained a little piece of Nothing, unnoticed in the darkness of the night sky. If you glimpse a place between the starts blacker than the emptiness of the night, that is the shadow of Nothing. This shadow was found by a celestial being named Suro, who claimed it and learned to wield its power to remain unseen and unnoticed when he wishes. His exploits became legend among the celestials of the daytime and nighttime realms.

However, all of the things in the night sky belong to Onnotangu, and when Lord Moon heard that one of his servants had claimed some unknown power for themselves, he had Suro brought before him. Suro denied having taken anything from Lord Moon's night, but he was not believed. Onnotangu called upon his consort, in whose court truth has power and whose servants can see and compel truthspeaking. It is unknown what price Lord Moon paid for this service, but his pride could not permit a member of his court to steal such power without paying him tribute.

Upon being brought before Lord Moon and demanded to account for what he had stolen under divine compulsion to speak only truth, Suro declare that he had taken Nothing. And so he was set free, and Lord Moon's wrath was turned upon the members of his heavenly court who had spread false rumors, but Suro requested that Lord Moon declare that none should suffer as a consequence of this matter, and Lord Moon granted his request as the aggrieved party.

Thus it came to pass that even after the source of Suro's power later became known, Lord Moon's decree that none should suffer continued to protect Suro, as punishing him would violate that decree, and Lord Moon's decree are absolute in the realm of night.

Disciples of Suro learn to call upon the power of the Shadow of Nothing by beseeching Suro to share this power with those who serve him. In the days before the Empire, this was done openly by those to the south of the Spine of the World mountains who later swore fealty to Bayushi, founder of the Scorpion clan. The Order of Lord Moon knows that the high priestess who was chosen by the Shinsei as the Scorpion clan Thunder chose her name in honor of Suro, which is why she was known as Shosuro. Indeed, after the defeat of Fu Leng, it was Shosuro alone who returned with the Black Scrolls, and though she died of her wounds shorly after, it was surely the power of the Shadow whose power allowed her to be the last surviving Thunder.
