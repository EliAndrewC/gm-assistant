# Design notes: Hayakawa County Magistracy

**Subject**: seat of County Magistrate Hida no Reiji Hajime - Hayakawa county, Nagahara province, Reiji domain (Crab Clan). A rich river-adjacent county UPSTREAM of Nagahara provincial city; most barges stop down there. Validation instance of the generic magistrate's-manor program (every knob deliberately set differently from Ochiba).

**Program type**: magistrate's manor (county magistracy) - see `buildings.md` "Compound programs".

**The magistrate** (from his Obsidian Portal record): Honor 1.5, XP 145 (young for the post), Permanent Wound, balding, judgmental, resentful about another's promotion, superstitious, sweaty. A low-honor incentive-driven official on one of the richest postings in the province, resentfully watching the barges glide past toward the prestigious city downstream. His synthesized backstory (in his OP GM-only notes, 2026-07, Claude-native): he was meant to sit the provincial ministry examinations the same spring the thaw-flood took the grain barge with his father (the previous magistrate) aboard; he leapt in after him and the current broke his leg against the revetment stones. He believes Hayakawa's wealth is borrowed from the river, which keeps its own ledger - his father died of arrears, so his bench punishes carelessness on the water savagely while coin settles dry-land cases. His cousin Hida no Reiji Fumito later took the ministry post he wanted (Governor Asuka never acknowledged his petition). The salt wards are Yasuki sea salt (never salt that touched the river), set by his own hand in strict order, main gate last - the night his father died, the landing-gate ward was found washed away though no rain had fallen. Full detail in the OP "Salt Wards" GM-only section.

## Knob settings

1. **Tier**: county magistracy (baseline).
2. **Posting wealth**: RICH - detached guest house, mature garden, fine timber. (The wealth shows in the residence side, not the bench: the hearing court itself is ordinary county size.)
3. **Granary weight**: STAGING NODE - single kura; the county's tax grain barges downstream to Nagahara city. Kura sits adjacent to tally office, postern, and landing for the shortest bale-haul.
4. **Garrison emphasis**: minimal (quiet rich county) - no watchtower, no armory; the river watch post at the landing is the security particular.
5. **Staff housing**: option (c) - senior retainers keep their own town houses; the compound barracks holds only the duty watch; ashigaru of the watch also lodge in town; heimen clerks commute. Resident on-grounds: Hajime + family (residence blocks), the karo's household (his own separate house in the residence court), and the ~10 domestic servants (cooks, grooms, cleaners, who bunk back-of-kitchen and stables, unlabeled per schematic convention). The karo does NOT lodge in the lord's wing - a chief retainer of any standing kept a separate dwelling (interior-audit correction 2026-07).
6. **Tenure character**: LINEAGE-HELD (GM ruling 2026-07: the Nagahara Reiji are a provincial lineage). The past magistrates are Hajime's own forebears - hence the ancestral alcove with predecessor tablets, and the accreted guest-wing annex "added by the 9th magistrate".
7. **Resident particulars**: superstitious Hajime keeps TWO well-tended modest shrines, salt wards at every door of the compound (main gate, kitchen postern, river door, landing gate, service gate) - a PAIR per door, OUTSIDE and flanking each opening (the ward keeps ill fortune from crossing the threshold, so it sits outside; 2026-07), and an enlarged bath (Permanent Wound + sweaty). No relics, no workshop, no dojo.
   - The **Fire Dragon shrine** (GM 2026-07, replacing the default Inari): dedicated to the Fire Dragon, one of the Five Elemental Dragons, the incarnate divinities of the elements (l7r.md). It was RETROFITTED from an Ebisu shrine, and a small Ebisu altar still keeps the corner - the working monks never fully evicted the Fortune of Honest Work. Why a river-fearing magistrate keeps fire burning at his shrine is left readable either way: fire against water, or a creditor's respect paid to the opposite power.
   - The **river-kami shrine**: offerings against flood and drowning, attended daily. The kami of the Hayakawa are PLURAL by doctrine (elemental kami are small and inhabit all things - every rapids, eddy, and ford has its own) but SINGULAR by address: the shrine enshrines them as one composite counterparty, addressed by the river's own borrowed name, "Hayakawa-no-kami" - the same folk move by which the Fox treat the whole Kitsune Mori as a singular "the wood". No one in the county claims to know a truer name. Hajime insists on the singular - a ledger needs one creditor.
8. **Justice-front furniture**: single hearing court, notice board. Nothing grander - Hajime's bench runs on incentives, not ceremony.

## The river logistics story

- Customs/tariffs are deliberately ABSENT: the Yasuki Taka tariff system collects at the provincial city gates downstream, NOT at county landings. This magistracy's river work is OUTBOUND - the tally office does barge manifests and seals; the river watch looks for gate-runners (bypass smuggling around Nagahara's tariff gates is the natural local crime).
- The tax barge is a HIRED town boatman's vessel under the magistracy's seals (offices owned no hulls); drawn at takasebune proportions (~47 x 7 ft).
- Stone revetment at the landing (swift river with a flood history - the river-kami shrine says so).
- The river band on the map is a margin crop of a wider river - tolerated; the drawn ~43 ft is the near-bank reach only.

## Deliberate choices and tolerated stretches

- Guest-wing annex bulges outside the main rectangle: accreted architecture, the tenure-character knob made visible.
- Guests' river door opens into the GUEST GARDEN (GM circulation rule 2026-07: guest doors feed courts, never building flanks); the kitchen postern deliberately opens into work space.
- Cell placement by the gate: free variable (grounding); guards double as jailers. Cell drawn ~2x real footprint: documented Scale glyph exemption (lattice legibility).
- Main gate opening 13.3 ft (was 27 ft; the size-audit agent caught the structure-vs-passage confusion, 2026-07). Gate hierarchy: main 13.3 > landing gate 12.7 (honest two-way cart minimum) > divider 8 (real nakamon) > posterns/doors ~9-11.
- The internal divider gate sits on the main axis directly behind the office hall: deliberate (the hall is the privacy baffle; formal visitors are received in the hall and go no deeper; heavy service uses the kitchen postern). Documented in the program's two-court bullet.
- The two modest shrines share the single approach torii that stands between them; they draw ~2x real (documented Scale exemption - interior glyphs need the room).
- The residence is drawn as TWO offset blocks (gankō / flying-geese massing) joined by a corridor, not a single 180-ft bar - real shoin residences massed as connected offset blocks (GM ruling 2026-07). Still generous overall (lineage accretion at a 9-generation posting), do not shrink.
- Residence room program: reception/zashiki (a residence keeps a formal reception room even with a separate office hall, per Takayama's yakutaku), Hajime's quarters + private study (shoin), family quarters, east rooms + ancestral alcove. Rooms are labeled by OCCUPANT rather than by historical function-name (okami/nakanoma/nando) - a deliberate GM schematic-legibility convention (2026-07); the underlying zones (lord's rooms, inner family rooms, the shoin study) are historically real.
- The karo has his own house in the residence court (west, below the bath), NOT a bay of the lord's wing - historical correction, GM ruling 2026-07.
- Each residence room-label is a multi-room ZONE/suite, not one room (a real residence of this footprint holds ~18-25 tatami rooms; compressed for legibility, a tolerated simplification per size-audit). The engawa strip on each block's garden face is the veranda that reads "residence, not nagaya." Block depth ~28 ft = the two-rows-plus-engawa section, on the shallow end but within range; the inner court can't fit deeper blocks alongside the garden/kitchen/shrine.
- The guest annex has NO privy: guests of rank receive chamber-pot service from the household staff (more courteous than sending an inspector across the garden at night); the inner-court latrine is household-side.
- No interrogation room: global rule (grounding) - do not flag.
- Glyph exemptions per buildings.md Scale: wells ~2x, kneeling marks ~2x, salt wards / lanterns / hearth pure glyph.
