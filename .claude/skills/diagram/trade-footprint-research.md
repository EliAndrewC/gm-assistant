# Trade footprints at map scale - which businesses outgrow the generic shop glyph

Research pass 2026-07-24 (GM question during the provincial-city audit follow-ups: "are there
trades whose real premises - attached works, yards, outbuildings - are materially bigger than a
shophouse, i.e. significant space our maps are not representing?"). Setting frame: Song/Ming
China first, Edo Japan second; city maps at 1 px = 3 ft draw commerce as generic ~48x32 ft
shop rowhouses + merchant kura + dedicated inn/stables/flophouse glyphs. **Status: findings
recorded, drawing decisions PENDING the GM (candidates listed at the end).**

## The shortlist - visibly bigger than a rowhouse at this scale

1. **Sake / miso / soy brewery (sakagura; Chinese zaofang) - the big one (attested).** Even a
   small county-seat brewer needed a vat hall with rows of 5-6 ft fermentation tanks, a koji
   room, a rice-steaming area with masonry kamado + chimney, its own well, and a rice kura.
   A minimal sakagura is a long gabled hall on the order of 60-120 ft x 30-40 ft BEHIND a
   normal shopfront - 3-8x the shophouse footprint - and surviving small-town compounds run to
   whole-block scale (one Nagano brewer: ~1,500 tsubo / ~53,000 sq ft, the wealthy end). It was
   very often the largest commercial building in a small town, rivaled only by the honjin or
   the largest inn. Siting: good well water mandatory; in-town (brewers were town elite).
   Expect 1-2 per seat of ~3,000.
2. **Dyer (kon-ya) - modest building, LARGE ground signature (attested).** The workshop (sunken
   indigo vats, boiling hearth) fits a deep shophouse; the DRYING does not - poles, racks, and
   rooftop platforms dominate the block in Hiroshige's Kanda Konya-cho print, and rinsing
   happened in the river (nunosarashi). Town scale: shophouse + a ~2,000-5,000 sq ft drying
   yard (bolts run ~35-40 ft) or riverbank frontage; dyers clustered on water.
3. **Lumber dealer (zaimokuya) - open riverside yard (attested pattern, size inferred).**
   Stacked timber + floated logs need an open yard (~3,000-8,000 sq ft at county-seat scale)
   with a river landing; edge of town near the downstream gate/wharf.
4. **Oil presser (aburaya / youfang) - a barn-scale works (attested for China).** The
   wedge-and-beam press is a massive timber machine (beam 15-30 ft) plus a seed-roasting stove
   and an ox-driven edge-runner mill on a ~20-25 ft circular track: a press barn ~40-60 ft x
   25-30 ft, fire-conscious, toward the edge.
5. **Pawnshop (shichiya) - ordinary shop, OVERSIZED storage (attested).** The tell is storage:
   pledges are bulky, so a prosperous pawnbroker kept 2-3 fireproof kura in a walled rear
   court - the existing kura glyph multiplied, not a new building type.
6. **Bathhouse (sento) - shophouse-scale building + a fuel yard (partially attested).** The
   bath is an ordinary streetfront building with a rear furnace + chimney; the visible extra is
   a ~1,000-2,000 sq ft firewood stack yard. Commercial bathhouses are China-first correct
   (attested from the Song on), so a city may keep 1-2.

## The null results (equally load-bearing)

- **NO FARRIERS (attested, important).** Edo Japan did not shoe horses - straw horse-sandals
  (umagutsu/umazouri) were the norm, iron shoeing arrived with Meiji; China trimmed hooves via
  village smiths. There is no big farrier-forge-with-stable-yard trade to miss: that image is a
  European import. A town blacksmith (kaji-ya) is a one-forge shophouse with at most a small
  side yard - the generic shop glyph is CORRECT for smiths. (If Unicorn/Moto horse culture
  shoes its steppe horses, that is a deliberate Moto-specific flavor question, not a city-map
  default.)
- **The long tail genuinely fits the shop glyph**: tofu maker, noodle/confection shops,
  apothecary, teahouse, cooper, tatami maker, carpenter's shop (his big work happens on-site),
  rice dealer (treadle-pestle hulling in-shop, grain in a rear kura).
- **Outside town entirely**: tile/pottery/charcoal/lime kilns (fire law + smoke push them to
  the periphery or nearby villages); tanneries/slaughter go downstream-riverside with the
  outside-the-walls burakumin quarter, never inside.

## Candidate map features (GM decision pending)

- A **brewery compound** feature (shopfront + long vat hall + kura + well, 1-2 per city) - the
  single biggest unrepresented structure; arguably the merchant-estate tier of workshops.
- A **dyer's drying yard** (rack-line yard or riverbank frontage, river cities especially).
- A **riverside lumber yard** (river cities; pairs naturally with Nagahara's wharf).
- An **oil-press barn** (1, toward the edge).
- Cheap wins within existing vocabulary: pawnbroker = 2-3 clustered kura behind one shop;
  sento = shop + woodpile; tile kiln as edge set-dressing; tannery as a note on the extramural
  burakumin quarter, not a drawn feature.
