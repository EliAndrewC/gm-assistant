Timeline of events specifically relating to the moon cult
The PCs belong to the Order of Lord Moon, which they conversationally refer to as “The Order”. (If they are ever overhead discussing “The Order”, they can easily claim that they are referring to The Order of Bishamon, to which they also belong.)

The Order of Lord Moon has operated in this area since before the dawn of the Empire, keeping secret prayers and rituals whose power is too limited to share. Like many of the secret sutras revealed by the Shinsei to the Hantei Emperor, some of the gifts of Lord Moon are distributed among all who perform the sacred rituals. The Order of therefore restricts its membership and perpetuates itself as a secret society so as to not be forced to reveal their hidden knowledge. If the rituals of The Order became universally available, Lord Moon’s blessings would be divided into so many small pieces as to be worthless to any individual worshiper. Through the centuries, The Order has found 12 to be an auspicious number of members, and strives to maintain exactly 12 disciples at all times.

Much like The Order itself, titles within The Order of Lord Moon have a full version and a less-incriminating abbreviated version. The full title is only used during sacred ceremonies and the abbreviated version is used at all other times. The head of The Order has the title The Master of the Lunar Way, but is referred to conversationally as “Master of the Way”, allowing members to claim that they are only referring to that person as a master of the Way of Shinsei.

1118
The Lion clan’s counterattack against the city after the spring thaw was massive but uncoordinated. Akodo Arasou launched an assault from all sides, using his massive advantage in numbers to force the defenders to fight at all city gates and at every possible point of entry.

When Lion forces broke through and established a bridgehead within the city, the Lion armies should have pulled back from their assault on other parts of the city walls to push forward into the city. However, there were many different Lion daimyo present, and none of them wanted to enter the city riding the coattails of a rival. Doji Chuto perceived this dynamic and did not order his men to fall back. Instead, he deployed all of his reserves to push the Lion back before they could secure their place, rather than pulling back to the inner city as most any other general would have done. This tactic was risky, but it succeeded at repelling the intruders and refortifying their defensive position.

Most of the other Lion daimyo present redoubled their efforts to breach the city’s defenses, far past the point where it was wise to do so, taking such heavy casualties that the entire operation ended in failure. Among those killed in this attempt was Akodo Arasao, the future daimyo of the Lion clan. After losing their general and taking such heavy losses, the Lion clan withdrew and the Crane clan kept the city.

With his brother dead, Akodo Toturi was named the next daimyo of the Lion clan, and he immediately began planning the next attempt to retake Toshi Ranbo. Akodo Kage, Revered Sensei of the Lion Clan, and personal karo to Akodo Toturi, spent the remainder of the year recruiting peasant operatives to infiltrate the city. They trickled in along with the other craftsmen and laborers who flock to the city looking for work, waiting for orders. Most were isolated and without direct knowledge of each other, but they were all led by Matsu Hiroru, a talented pupil of Akodo Kage and brother to Matsu Tsuko, daimyo of the Matsu family.

During this time, the city’s granaries and storehouses were largely emptied after being strained by the city’s defense, and this led to riots and demonstrations by the peasantry, which prompted governor Tsume Yasujiro to declare martial law. Prince Hantei Fukuhachi, the Emperor’s youngest adult son, was assigned to oversee the transition and provide Imperial oversight to the city’s administration. Prince Fukuhachi was tasked primarily with ensuring that order was maintained, and that further uprisings were not permitted in the Emperor’s third-largest city, a mere few hundred miles from Otosan Uchi.

The Tsume mainly left alone the monks and priests who maintain the favor of the gods (beyond plundering the temples for their treasuries). Haruki was Master of the Way during this period, though his official position within the Order of Bishamon was the Warden of the Five Gates. As a non-samurai monk he retained his position under Crane rule, as did most other members of The Order. However, every political post in the Ministry of Rites was filled with Tsume candidates, including the positions held by non-samurai.

1119
The prophet Baiten was largely unknown at this time in Toshi Ranbo, though he had developed quite a following in Phoenix lands. At the beginning of 1119, Baiten was spreading his ministry through Dragonfly lands to the northwest of Toshi Ranbo, but many of his disciples came to the city to spread his teachings.

One of Baiten’s followers was a man named Hideo who started a group devoted to educating peasants in religious songs and chants. After much discontent over the harsh treatment of the city’s populace, Hideo eventually made a public speech in which he possibly said and/or implied (reports vary about precisely what was said) that Prince Fukuhachi would be like the next Iuchiban, dividing the Empire and aiding the forces of darkness through immoral and unjust actions.

Hideo’s followers denounced the speech and claimed to have had no idea he would give it. Prince Fukuhachi still had every member of the group executed, which was followed by significant additional unrest. After this incident, Prince Fukuhachi appointed a Board of Justice composed of 5 Imperial Magistrates drawn from other clans to oversee Toshi Ranbo’s 5 districts. These magistrates were sent for at the end of the year and arrived the following spring.

Towards the end of the year, Tsume Retsu, the man who had conquered Toshi Ranbo, was murdered under mysterious circumstances. His killer has never been found.

1120
The three most noteworthy (to The Order) members of the Imperial Board of Justice which reported to Prince Fukuhachi were Shinjo Jotsu, Yasuki Suzuki, and Otaku Taira.

When the prophet Baiten eventually came to the city, he drew enormous crowds – tens of thousands of peasants would gather to hear him preach. The Tsume city government was concerned about the potential for additional unrest, and it is said that they considered having him executed but were stopped by Prince Fukuhachi. Suzuki attempted to have Baiten assassinated, but the attempt failed, with the assassins killed by the crowd before they could be questioned, and Suzuki’s involvement was only later discovered. The unrest which followed as many of Baiten’s followers blamed the city for this attempt led to Baiten being exiled by governor Yasujiro.

Meanwhile, the political officials who came into the new provincial Ministry of Rites were overwhelmed with their new responsibilities. While they were experienced at all the usual formulaic prayers and celebrations and festivals, many of the local rites and practices fell by the wayside. This angered the ancestral god of the Damasu during the pivotal time when the distances between the realms are at their lowest and the barriers at their weakest.

Ishikawa was a member of The Order, who also served in the Order of Bishamon as the Champion of the Sparring Circle. As the senior disciple of Ryoshun, Ishikawa was visited in a dream by Ryoshun while he was outside the city, who revealed that the ancestral god of the Damasu had allowed him into the domain. Ryoshun required additional permission to be granted by an Imperial authority in order to enter Toshi Ranbo, as per the compacts between Lord Moon and Lady Sun.

Ishikawa relayed the instructions he had been given as to how to open the way for Ryoshun to approach a member of the Board of Justice. Two members of the Imperial Board of Justice (Yasuki Suzuki and Otaku Taira) would become afflicted by the Wasting Disease. Taira would travel north to Dragon lands to be healed by the prophet Baiten, while Suzuki would race to Phoenix lands, accompanied by his friend Shinjo Jotsu, to seek the aid of the Council of Elemental Masters. In repayment for saving his life, Suzuki would go Walking the Way into the realm of dreams to retrieve an artifact of great power for Isawa Tadaka, the Elemental Master of Earth. At the appointed time, the four most senior members of The Order entered a shared dream to open the way for Ryoshun, as instructed, to approach Suzuki and strike a bargain with him.

Suzuki left the city shortly thereafter, and his companion Jotsu appeared to betray him upon his departure. He revealed to the city government that Suzuki had struck a bargain with a servant of Lord Moon, granting members of Lord Moon’s heavenly court entry into the city of Toshi Ranbo for a period of 3 years. Prince Fukuhachi wrote an order rescinding this permission, but when he attempted to sign and seal the decree, his Imperial signet ring left no mark on the melted wax.

During this time, members of The Order felt their powers and abilities strengthening like never before. What had previously been the pinnacle of mastery was now recategorized as The First Phase of a larger set of gifts. The Order was clearly being rewarded for their efforts, even if Suzuki’s position in the city was at an end.

Having also returned from being healed by the prophet Baiten, Otaku Taira shared the revelation that the next Day of Thunder was at hand. He declared that his own dreams had been interpreted by the prophet, and that Governor Yasujiro should be replaced. Taira fought and won a duel against Yasujiro’s yojimbo, killing Tsume Koreshiba, who had been known as the greatest surviving duelist of the Tsume family. Governor Yasujiro stepped down and was replaced by Tsume Momoko, a district lieutenant who was chosen over dozens of more senior candidates at the urging of the Board of Justice.

During this time, a bloodspeaker whose name is forbidden to speak but who all locals know to be named Jama Manabu began targeting well-known nobles in the city. His most widely-known early exploit was when he attempted to kidnap Tsume Horibinu, the county magistrate of Hokobayashi, a short distance outside the city.

With the local Witch Hunter in active pursuit of Manabu, Prince Fukuhachi set out with Board of Justice members Shinjo Jotsu, Otaku Taira, and Shosuro Ajo to visit the prophet Baiten and consult with him about how to expel the servants of Lord Moon from the city. While he was gone, The Order proceeded to remove all of their ceremonial implements and costumes from the city to make themselves in compliance with the new decree that the worship of Lord Moon shall not be permitted within the walls of Toshi Ranbo.

The specifics of Prince Fukuhachi’s meeting with the prophet Baiten are heavily disputed. Baiten’s counsel to the Prince was so upsetting that Prince Fukuhachi’s yojimbo took umbrage and attacked Baiten, which resulted in a brawl. Otaku Taira defended the prophet, but in so doing he struck the Prince, and was later stripped of his family name and exiled as a ronin for having done so. Rumors abound in Toshi Ranbo to this day about what really occurred, but immediately afterwards Prince Fukuhachi was assigned the position of General of the 1st Imperial Legion, where to this day he guards the Gateway to the Land of the Burning Sands. His older brother Hantei Daigotsu, the Emperor’s second son and previous general of the 1st Legion, took over Prince Fukuhachi’s position as Imperial overseer of Toshi Ranbo.

While this dispute was being handled, Jama Manabu struck again, murdering Doji Chuto and using his name at the moment of his death to recall Oni no Manabu, which proceeded to ravage the city, killing its victims by the hundreds and wounding them by the thousands. Upon his return to the city, Shinjo Jotsu proceeded to track down Manabu an take him by surprise, beheading him in the middle of what appeared to be a routine interview of many local peasants. Manabu’s identity as a bloodspeaker was confirmed by the previously-hidden taint in his body no longer being concealed in death.

Prince Hantei Daigotsu summoned Baiten to the city and tasked him with expelling the servants of Lord Moon. Baiten made many demands of the city government, but in the end a great ritual was planned, with hundreds of monks and priests in and out of the city leading tens of thousands in prayer. Members of The Order arranged to lead peasants in the countryside in prayer so that no one from The Order would be present in the city while this ritual was being performed, lest they be struck down by its power.

At the culmination of the ritual, the city gates of Toshi Ranbo were blown open and severely damaged as the members of Lord Moon’s heavenly court were expelled from the city. In the hours after the ritual, rogue city magistrates set upon the prophet and murdered him – it was years later before they were discovered to have been acting at the direction of Shinjo Jotsu.

The city gates were quickly repaired, but the repair work focused on the ability to repel a Lion assault from outside the city. The agents in the city recruited by Akodo Kago and coordinated by his pupil Matsu Hiroru were able to attack the city gates from within and sabotage them just ahead of the second Lion invasion. This time Akodo Toturi struck just after the first major snowfall had melted, and with the aid of the carefully planned sabotage his forces were able to easily overwhelm the city’s defenses a few days into the new year. Governor Tsume Momoku was killed, along with her new husband Doji Hirota and most of the high ranking Tsume and Doji nobles in the city.

1121
The Order found their powers continuing to grow, even with Ryoshun having been expelled from the city. The Order continued to pray for guidance, but none came. By the time any member of The Order attained the Third Phase as a Disciple of Goju, Suzuki was too far from the city to be able to track.

The members of The Order contemplated sending their senior Disciple of Goju on a pilgrimage around the Empire in an attempt to find Suzuki. After all, it was he who Ryoshun had approached, and it was by his hand that members of Lord Moon’s heavenly court had been granted access to Toshi Ranbo. Perhaps finding him would allow them to continue their path in Lord Moon’s service. But with it being unclear where to look for him, and without a particular plan as to what to do if he was found, The Order decided not to split their membership on such an extended and uncertain journey.

1122
While it took over a year for the Lion/Crane war to officially begin, those in Toshi Ranbo could see it coming long before it was made official just after the spring thaw of this year.

1123
Shortly before the Day of Thunder, the descendant of Shinsei Grand Abbot Okawa discerned that crystal would be the key to a new technique by which moon cultists could be detected. Though he died before he could develop the particulars of the technique, he sent word of this revelation with a trusted messenger just before the final confrontation with Shinjo Jotsu, who was revealed on the Day of Thunder to be the true inheritor of the Moon Cult, wielding the power of Lord Moon against the Seven Thunders. (Also killed was Hida O-Ushi, the Crab Thunder and daughter of Hida Kisada, the daimyo of the Crab clan.)

This came only weeks after moon cultists in the Scorpion Clan murdered their daimyo Bayushi Shoju in an attempted coup. They were thwarted by his brother Bayushi Aramoro, whose agents had only just begun infiltrating the cult when they sprung their coup.

Jotsu was ultimately killed on the Day of Thunder, and shortly afterwards the bloodspeaker Jama Kohaku appeared on the scene, declaring himself to have been delayed and unable to fight alongside Jotsu as planned. However, he did kill the surviving Thunders, including the Unicorn Thunder Otaku Taira, whose family name was restored posthumously. Kohaku declared that all loyal members of the moon cult should retreat to Kyuden Shinjo, where the moon cult remained strong.

1124
Shinjo Hanari quickly overthrew Shinjo Yokatsu as soon as word of Jama Kohaku’s declaration reached him, executing the former daimyo for high treason against the Empire and declaring a blood debt against his family line. Because the technique of moon cultist detection was not yet known, Hanari had no way of knowing who was a cultist, and like Bayushi Aramoro before him, he reacted by killing anyone who could possibly be a cultist, also killing or exiling their families.

However, there were some moon cultists which both Hanari and Aramoro kept alive. These were turned over to the Kuni Witch Hunters for experimentation. Kuni Yori devised various procedures to uncover their weaknesses in order to develop a means by which someone could be tested for whether they were a moon cultist. The only reliable, useful test which has been widely disseminated is to focus sunlight through crystal into a person’s eye long enough to blind them in that eye – in moon cultists, this blackens the eye throughout, including in spots which were not touched by the focused sunlight.

1125
Kuni Yori’s efforts were successful, and he sent word to the Hantei Emperor of his findings that a moon cultist could be detected by focusing sunlight through a crystal to burn out one of their eyes. While this would strike anyone blind, it would cause an additional blackening of the eyeball which would reveal a moon cultist for what they are.

It turned out that Kuni Yori was successful at this in part because of a partnership he struck with moon cultists who he had captured. Emperor Hantei the 16th, the Steel Chrysanthemum, had granted the Jade Champion the right to practice maho, in order to better learn the ways of the enemies of the Empire. This Imperial decree was later rescinded, but Kuni Yori possessed an original copy of the order, and used it to claim the right to take the names of the moon cultists on whom he was experimenting.

Able to track and manipulate them using the power over them provided by his possession of their names, Kuni Yori traveled with these moon cultists, disguised as his servants. He brought them on his mission from the Emperor to recharge the Black Scrolls, during which he revealed his copy of Hantei the 16th’s Imperial decree to those under his command. The bushi brought on this mission were not fooled, and they immediately executed him. However, they were unaware that his servants were moon cultists, who were now freed from his control and once again in possession of their own names, and these moon cultists slipped away from Ranpo Temple before they could be discovered for what they were.

The Emperor proceeded to appoint his second son, Hantei Daigotsu, to head the new Office of the Crystal Champion, which was tasked with hunting moon cultists. The roles and responsibilities of this office are yet are still not yet fully defined, as the Crystal Charter has not yet been written.

At Imperial Winter Court later that year, Iuchi Shahai revealed herself to be a moon cultist and attempted to assassinate the Emperor. She managed to escape, though her plan failed, and the Emperor was able to successfully broker lasting peace treaties between the warring Lion/Unicorn and Lion/Crane factions.

Iuchi Shahai’s coven and the captives of Kuni Yori are the two main groups of treasonous moon cultists which are known to be at large in the Empire today.

1128
In the aftermath of the attempted assassination of the Emperor, Toshi Ranbo came under increased scrutiny, as it was known as the birthplace of the moon cult. It had been previously assumed that Jotsu brought any confederates with him when he left the city, but after Iuchi Shahai demonstrated how active the cult still was, a renewed fear of lingering cultists gripped the city.

The first act of Hantei Daigotsu in his capacity as the first Crystal Champion was to come to Toshi Ranbo with Matsu Gohei in order to ferret out any possible moon cultists. Daigotsu worked to attempt to map out everyone who had contact with Shinjo Jotsu, as well as who fit the profile for having the powers known to be possessed by moon cultists.

The Order hoped that they would evade scrutiny due to the fact that no member of The Order ever interacted with Jotsu. Unfortunately, Ishikawa, Champion of the Sparring Circle of the Temple of Bishamon, was a disciple of Ryoshun, and his reputation was his downfall. He was “put to the test” (his eye was burned out with crystal) and revealed as a moon cultist.

Many other members of the Order of Bishamon were immediately put to the test, including the Master of the Lunar Way (head of The Order), Kaito, who had been serving as the Master of the Inner Sanctum of the Temple of Bishamon. Interestingly, another member of The Order, Natsuo, was put to the test due to her closeness with Master Kaito, and she passed the test.

It appears that members of The Order who have attained only the Crescent Moon Phase of their discipleship of any of Lord Moon’s servants will pass a crystal test, whereas members who have reached the Gibbous Moon Phase will not. It is unclear whether a half-moon phase would exceed whatever threshold causes the eye to blacken in the characteristic manner which gives away a cultist.

Kaito and Ishikawa were both taken away and questioned, but The Order had previously discussed what story to tell in this situation, and they both clearly stuck to their prepared tale of having been recruited by Jotsu and not aware of any other cultists in the city. Though in all likelihood they were likely promised that they would not be executed if they gave up additional moon cultists, a promise made credible by other moon cultists having previously spent long years in captivity, they remained loyal to The Order, and no other members were betrayed to the Crystal Champion, and both Kaito and Ishikawa were executed.

In the aftermath of losing the two highest-ranking members of The Order, the powers of the remaining members immediately increased by a noticeable amount. The Order had always limited its membership due to the limited nature of the blessings of Lord Moon, and it appears that even with the recent elevation in the powers bestowed, this dynamic is still very much in play.

The Order debated internally about whether to continue to induct new members. There have been exactly 12 members for centuries, 12 is an auspicious number, many of the rituals performed by The Order require exactly 12 participants, and thus it was unclear whether limiting the membership would outweigh the potential downsides. However, this was contentious, and The Order was split on the issue, though a slim majority were eventually swayed to maintain tradition by Reiji, the new Master of the Lunar Way.

In the aftermath of multiple “moon cultists” having been found in the Order of Bishamon, many senior members of the temple were dismissed from their posts. This caused another debate within The Order about whether members should pursue advancement within the ranks of the Sovereign Temple of Bishamon. The more remarkable your gifts and talents, the more suspicion you might draw. It was eventually decided that individual members could choose to pursue such advancement if they wished, but The Order would not push any individual members to seek promotions.

Master Reiji chose to seek promotion and became the Warden of the Five Gates of the Temple of Bishamon. The PCs may or may not have achieved promotion during this time – this will be determined at character creation.

Apart from this entire affair, a dueling tournament was held at the end of the year according to the terms of the peace treaty between the Lion and Crane, which determined the final ownership of several disputed provinces. The Lion did well for themselves overall, though 1 province in particular was lost in a surprise upset due to Doji Masayo, who appeared at the tournament with the cursed sword Shitsuten.

1129
The fungal blight kumosaya struck the domain. Initially it did not appear that it would have great effect, but ultimately it destroyed half of the rice and soybean crops, devastating the domain and causing much hardship, especially among the peasantry, many of whom fell deeply into debt due to being unable to pay their debts.

The Order of Bishamon has been asked by the Damasu chancellary to look into why this happened, and why most of the omens and portents did not warn the administration of a pending catastrophe.
