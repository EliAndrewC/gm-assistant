# Clans, Families, and Schools

This is a curated summary of clan, family, and school character, faithful to the
original L5R source books on which L7R is based and to the generator's own
family/school configuration. It exists to give a synthesized character a
concrete cultural starting point. It is a working aid, not canon - the canonical
record is l7r.md.

## The 7 Great Clans

**Lion Clan** - the right hand of the Emperor and the Empire's foremost army.
Martial, honor-bound, tradition-obsessed, and proud to the point of rigidity.
Families: **Akodo** (the ruling family, master strategists and disciplined
generals), **Matsu** (a vast, fierce, famously hot-blooded warrior family with a
celebrated berserker tradition), **Ikoma** (bards, historians, and storytellers
who keep the clan's glory alive), **Kitsu** (a small, semi-mystical family of
priests and keepers of ancestral lore). Schools: Akodo bushi, Matsu berserker,
Ikoma bard.

**Crane Clan** - the left hand of the Emperor, arbiters of art, courtesy, and
the duel. Refined, beautiful, politically formidable, and convinced they are the
custodians of culture itself. Families: **Doji** (the ruling family, peerless
courtiers and administrators), **Kakita** (artisans and the deadliest duelists
in the Empire, masters of the fast-draw iaijutsu strike), **Daidoji** (the
pragmatic "Iron Crane" soldiers and guardians who do the unglamorous work the
others will not), **Asahina** (pacifist artisan-priests). Schools: Kakita
duelist, Daidoji bushi, Doji artisan/courtier.

**Crab Clan** - the Empire's shield, who hold the Kaiu Wall against the
Shadowlands so the rest of Rokugan can afford its refinements. Blunt, practical,
hard-drinking, contemptuous of courtly nonsense, and grimly competent. Families:
**Hida** (the ruling family, massive frontline warriors), **Hiruma** (scouts and
rangers who fight beyond the Wall), **Kuni** (witch hunters and shugenja who
study the taint), **Kaiu** (engineers and smiths who built the Wall), **Yasuki**
(merchants and quartermasters, defectors from the Crane long ago, who fund the
whole grim enterprise). Schools: Hida bushi, Hiruma bushi.

**Scorpion Clan** - the Emperor's "Underhand," who do the dishonorable things the
Empire needs done and bear the infamy for it. Spies, blackmailers, manipulators,
and loyalists who wear masks literally and figuratively. Families: **Bayushi**
(the ruling family, schemers and courtiers), **Shosuro** (actors, infiltrators,
and poisoners), **Soshi** (shugenja), **Yogo** (cursed to betray those they love;
wards and defensive magic). Schools: Bayushi bushi, Yogo bushi, Shosuro actor.

**Unicorn Clan** - returned generations ago from centuries riding the lands
beyond the Empire, carrying foreign (gaijin) ways that still unsettle their
neighbors. Cavalry without equal, plain-spoken, and only half-trusted. Families:
**Shinjo** (the ruling family, explorers and riders), **Moto** (fierce, partly
of the Burning Sands, with their own gods and customs), **Otaku** (the elite
battle-maidens and their war-horses; note this setting spells it Otaku, not
Utaku), **Ide** (diplomats and merchants who smooth the way), **Iuchi**
(shugenja with foreign-flavored magic). Schools: Shinjo bushi, Otaku battle
maiden, Ide diplomat.

**Dragon Clan** - remote mountain mystics, inscrutable and few, who speak in
riddles and pursue enlightenment by their own paths. Families: **Togashi** (the
ruling family, tattooed ise zumi monks touched by the clan's founder),
**Mirumoto** (the clan's soldiers, masters of the two-sword style), **Kitsuki**
(magistrates and investigators who pioneered evidence-based inquiry), **Agasha**
(shugenja and alchemists). Schools: Mirumoto two-sword bushi, Kitsuki
magistrate, Togashi ise zumi.

**Phoenix Clan** - the soul of the Empire and the masters of magic, peace-loving
and reluctant to make war, but holding the greatest concentration of shugenja in
Rokugan. Families: **Shiba** (the ruling family, who rule but defer to the
Isawa in matters of the spirit; their bushi guard the shugenja), **Isawa**
(the most powerful shugenja family, proud and inward-looking), **Asako**
(scholars and philosophers). Schools: Shiba bushi, Isawa shugenja/bushi.

## The Imperial Families

The **Hantei** (the Imperial line itself), **Seppun** (who guard the Emperor's
person and soul), **Otomo** (who keep the Great Clans divided and weak so none
threatens the throne), and **Miya** (heralds and messengers who carry the
Emperor's word). They serve the Throne directly rather than any clan.

## Minor Clans

Small clans, usually founded for a single purpose and far below the Great Clans
in prestige. Present in this campaign region:

- **Wasp Clan** (the **Tsuruchi** family) - a clan of bounty hunters chartered
  relatively recently, a mix of Scorpion and Crane samurai together with a large
  share of **peasantborn** samurai elevated for raw skill. Meritocratic,
  hard-edged, and chip-on-the-shoulder; they earn their swords rather than
  inheriting them, and the older clans look down on them for it. Famous for the
  bow.
- **Fox Clan** (the **Kitsune** family) - a tiny, rustic clan living close to a
  haunted forest, known for wardens, woodland priests, and an easy familiarity
  with fox-spirits and the supernatural that their neighbors find unnerving.
- **Sparrow Clan** (the **Suzume** family) - poor, proud, and famously
  hospitable; they have little but their honor and their word.
- Also present in smaller numbers: the **Mantis** (Shione), **Dragonfly**
  (Tonbo), and **Hare** (Usagi).

## Schools and roles (any clan)

Beyond the clan-specific schools above, a samurai's training points to how they
move through the world:

- **bushi** - a warrior, the default samurai (~80% of the caste). Trained in
  arms; their duty is to fight and to lead.
- **courtier** / **diplomat** - a samurai of words, who fights with etiquette,
  favors, marriages, and information rather than steel.
- **merchant** - a samurai who manages money and trade for their lord, a
  necessary but faintly disreputable specialty (handling money is half-people's
  work), most associated with the Yasuki and Ide.
- **shugenja** / **priest** - one who beseeches the kami; rare, revered, and
  the closest thing to an open practitioner of the supernatural.
- **peasantborn** - not a school but an origin: a samurai raised from the heimen
  caste, common among the Wasp and other minor clans. Carries both the gratitude
  and the stigma of low birth, and rarely the polish of those raised to it.
