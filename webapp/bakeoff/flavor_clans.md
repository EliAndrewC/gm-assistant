# The Clans, Families, and Schools

Each Clan's character below is the downstream consequence of its geography,
resources, neighbors, and distance from the capital - not an innate temperament
carried in noble blood. Every Clan administers land, levies troops, collects
taxes, and survives within the same feudal system and macroclimate, so the Clans
are far more similar than they are different. The familiar stereotypes (the
martial Lion, the refined Crane, the underhanded Scorpion) are real but
reductive: treat them as explanations, not labels to apply by rote. A character's
own traits and history always outrank the reputation of their Clan.

## The 7 Great Clans

**Crab Clan** - hold the southern frontier, where the Kaiu Wall stands between
the Empire and the Shadowlands. Their land is the Empire's poorest - marsh,
mountain, and tainted ground that yields little rice - and their defining cost is
permanent: the Wall must be manned, supplied, and rebuilt without pause, and
raids carried into the Shadowlands to keep the pressure off it. Almost everything
attributed to the Crab follows from that. They keep the Empire's most streamlined
system for levying and rotating troops through tours on the Wall; they have no
surplus for courtly refinement and openly disdain those who do; and their
families are the division of labor the frontier demands: **Hida** (the ruling
family, frontline warriors), **Hiruma** (scouts and rangers who fight beyond the
Wall), **Kuni** (witch hunters and shugenja who study the taint they live
beside), **Kaiu** (engineers and smiths who built and maintain the Wall), and
**Yasuki** (merchants and quartermasters, Crane defectors long ago, whose trade
funds an enterprise their own land cannot pay for). Schools: Hida bushi, Hiruma
bushi.

**Crane Clan** - hold the fertile coastal plains of the west, the richest
farmland and busiest trade in the Empire, long-settled and close enough to the
capital to shape its politics. Wealth and proximity, not numbers or steel, are
their advantage, so they invest in the things that convert money into influence:
art, etiquette, marriage, administration, and the formal duel as an instrument of
policy. Their reputation for refinement is the visible surface of an economic and
political strategy. Families: **Doji** (the ruling family, courtiers and
administrators), **Kakita** (artisans and duelists, who made the fast-draw
iaijutsu strike a refined institution that settles disputes without armies),
**Daidoji** (the pragmatic "Iron Crane" soldiers who do the unglamorous defensive
work the clan's wealth depends on), **Asahina** (pacifist artisan-priests).
Schools: Kakita duelist, Daidoji bushi, Doji artisan/courtier.

**Lion Clan** - hold the broad central plains, populous and open, bordering more
neighbors than any other Clan. Open ground good for fielding large infantry
armies, a big population to raise them from, and many contested borders to defend
make them the Empire's standing army by circumstance as much as by charter. A
large army needs cohesion and legitimacy more than it needs clever tactics, which
is why the Lion invest so heavily in history, lineage, and honor as social
technology - the machinery that makes soldiers march and obey. Families:
**Akodo** (the ruling family, strategists and disciplined generals), **Matsu** (a
vast warrior family with a celebrated berserker tradition), **Ikoma** (bards and
historians who keep the clan's glory - and its claims - alive), **Kitsu** (a
small family of priests and keepers of ancestral lore). Schools: Akodo bushi,
Matsu berserker, Ikoma bard.

**Scorpion Clan** - hold a small, hot, dry domain in the southwest, cut off from
the populous Lion by the Spine of the World Mountains and lying about as far from
the capital as the Empire reaches. A small Clan that cannot win a conventional
war against larger neighbors needs other leverage, and its land supplies it: the
region grows opium unusually well, and its remoteness makes it a natural home for
the vices the rest of the Empire pretends not to need. Out of that grew an
economy and a craft of secrets - smuggling, gambling, blackmail, intelligence,
and courtly manipulation - and the reputation for underhandedness the Clan has
learned to wear deliberately. Their trade city, Ryoko Owari, is the Empire's
clearing-house for both opium and information. Families: **Bayushi** (the ruling
family, schemers and courtiers), **Shosuro** (actors, infiltrators, and
poisoners), **Soshi** (shugenja), **Yogo** (wards and defensive magic, cursed to
betray those they love). Schools: Bayushi bushi, Yogo bushi, Shosuro actor.

**Unicorn Clan** - returned generations ago from centuries spent riding the open
lands beyond the Empire, and hold western plains and grazing country suited to
raising horses at scale. Open range makes cavalry the natural core of their
armies, and their long absence makes their customs, gods, and even their
bloodlines half-foreign to their neighbors - hence the lingering mistrust. Years
on the trade roads of the wider world also left them with diplomats and merchants
used to dealing across borders. Families: **Shinjo** (the ruling family,
explorers and riders), **Moto** (fierce, partly of the Burning Sands, with their
own gods and customs), **Otaku** (the elite battle-maidens and their war-horses;
this setting spells it Otaku, not Utaku), **Ide** (diplomats and merchants who
smooth the way), **Iuchi** (shugenja with foreign-flavored magic). Schools:
Shinjo bushi, Otaku battle maiden, Ide diplomat.

**Phoenix Clan** - hold the cold, mountainous northeast, defensible and sparsely
populated, with little fertile land and little to gain by conquest. A Clan with
hard borders to cross and few spoils to fight over has every reason to avoid war
and few reasons to start one, which is the material root of their pacifism. What
their land does concentrate is shugenja: the Isawa hold the densest body of
priestly talent in the Empire, and the Clan's power and identity rest on the
spirit world rather than the battlefield. Families: **Shiba** (the ruling family,
who govern but defer to the Isawa in spiritual matters; their bushi guard the
shugenja), **Isawa** (the most powerful shugenja family, proud and
inward-looking), **Asako** (scholars and philosophers). Schools: Shiba bushi,
Isawa shugenja/bushi.

**Dragon Clan** - hold the far northern mountains, the highest and least arable
land in the Empire, which supports only a small population in scattered,
self-sufficient communities. Isolation and poverty breed few people and
idiosyncratic institutions: cut off from the rest of the Empire and from easy
outside scrutiny, the Dragon pursue their own paths and owe their character to
that distance more than to any mystery of blood. Their reputation for
inscrutability is, in plain terms, what a small mountain society becomes when it
is left mostly alone. Families: **Togashi** (the ruling family, tattooed ise zumi
monks touched by the clan's founder), **Mirumoto** (soldiers and masters of the
two-sword style suited to broken mountain terrain), **Kitsuki** (magistrates who
pioneered evidence-based investigation), **Agasha** (shugenja and alchemists).
Schools: Mirumoto two-sword bushi, Kitsuki magistrate, Togashi ise zumi.

## The Imperial Families

The **Hantei** (the Imperial line itself), **Seppun** (who guard the Emperor's
person and soul), **Otomo** (who keep the Great Clans divided so none threatens
the throne), and **Miya** (heralds who carry the Emperor's word). They serve the
Throne directly rather than any Clan, and their roles all manage the same problem
from the center: a throne that rules many large, armed Clans must keep them
balanced against one another.

## Minor Clans

Small clans, usually chartered for a single purpose and far below the Great Clans
in land and prestige. Present in this campaign region:

- **Wasp Clan** (the **Tsuruchi** family) - chartered relatively recently and
  holding little hereditary land or standing, so they trade on the one thing they
  can sell: skill. They hire out as bounty hunters and recruit on merit,
  including a large share of **peasantborn** samurai elevated for raw ability,
  which the older Clans look down on. Famous for the bow. Their chip-on-the-
  shoulder meritocracy is what a Clan with no inherited prestige does to survive.
- **Fox Clan** (the **Kitsune** family) - a tiny, rustic Clan living beside a
  haunted forest. Their wardens, woodland priests, and easy familiarity with
  fox-spirits and the supernatural come from sharing a border with it, and
  unnerve neighbors who do not.
- **Sparrow Clan** (the **Suzume** family) - poor, holding marginal land that
  yields little, they have made their name on hospitality and on keeping their
  word, which is the kind of capital available to those without rice or soldiers.
- Also present in smaller numbers: the **Mantis** (Shione), **Dragonfly**
  (Tonbo), and **Hare** (Usagi).

## Schools and roles (any clan)

Beyond the clan-specific schools above, a samurai's training points to how they
move through the world:

- **bushi** - a warrior, the default samurai (~80% of the caste). Trained in
  arms; their duty is to fight and to lead.
- **courtier** / **diplomat** - a samurai of words, who fights with etiquette,
  favors, marriages, and information rather than steel.
- **merchant** - a samurai who manages money and trade for their lord, a
  necessary but faintly disreputable specialty (handling money is half-people's
  work), most associated with the Yasuki and Ide.
- **shugenja** / **priest** - one who beseeches the kami; rare, revered, and
  the closest thing to an open practitioner of the supernatural.
- **peasantborn** - not a school but an origin: a samurai raised from the heimen
  caste, common among the Wasp and other minor clans. Carries both the gratitude
  and the stigma of low birth, and rarely the polish of those raised to it.
