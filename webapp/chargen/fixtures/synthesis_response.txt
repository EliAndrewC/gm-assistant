Tsuruchi Hideki looks more like a highwayman than a high-ranking veteran of Shiro Reiji. He wears a thick beard and keeps his head shaved bare on the left side, leaving a ragged topknot to hang over the right. His uniform is a functional collection of frayed seams and muddy hems. Born a peasant, Hideki never adopted the refined habits of the samurai caste. He is a boisterous man who drinks with wagon drivers and laughs loudly at his own mistakes. This lack of pretense makes him a favorite among the rural population, but it earns him only scorn from his peers. Hideki remains genuinely humble, viewing himself as nothing more than a sturdy prop to keep a roof from caving in on his betters.

His terrible reputation and absolute lack of honor stem from this lowborn pragmatism. Hideki treats bushido as a dangerous distraction on the road. He also refuses to observe orthodox religious rites, preferring to pour cheap sake over specific roadside rocks to bribe nameless earth spirits for clear weather. During an escort mission in the Month of the Rooster, his caravan was ambushed by archers. Hideki threw a magistrate's ancestral banner into a ditch so he could use both hands to drag the wounded official to cover. He saved his client's life, but the magistrate formally complained to the Ami lineage about Hideki's spiritual contamination and cowardice. Hideki simply agreed with the insults, apologized, and went back to guarding the perimeter.
