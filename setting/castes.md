CASTES:

Rokugan has a strict caste system, which is part of the Celestial Order.  The Hantei Emperor is the Son of Heaven, descended from the Sun and Moon, and owns all of the land everywhere in the world, though gaijin barbarians fail to recognize his authority, so taxes are collected within the Emerald Empire.  The samurai (known as "those who make war" or "those who serve") make up the ruling class, and are the only caste with full personhood - samurai are "people", and this is why we often refer to "inhabitants" or "humans" when talking about populations.

Heimen are "half-people" and include farmers and merchants.

Hinin are "non-people" and laborers, servants, and entertainers.  The lowest part of the hinin caste are the eta, or "untouchables" who perform ritually unclean labor such as mortians and tanners and those who handle "night-soil" (human feces).

Monks exist outside the Celestial Order, walking as the Shinsei did with one foot in this world and one foot in the spiritual realm.  Although monks are a small part of the population (less than 1%) they are enormously influential due to the reverence held by humans of all castes for the gods and their servants and those who preach the Tao of Shinsei.

Here's a list of some example professions and subsets of the lower castes:

Hinin:
-> Entertainers, including these examples:
    Wrestlers
    Musicians
    Restraunteurs
    Actors
    Masseurs
    Sex Workers (prostitution is illegal in Rokugan by Imperial decree but such laws are "honored more in breach than observance")
    "Carnies" (Jesters, Jugglers, Magicians, etc)
-> Beggars
-> Laborers, especially these examples:
    Porters
    Servants
    Palequin Carriers
    Cooks
    Ditch Diggers
    Charcoal burners
    Miners
    Salt burners
    Quarrying
    Basketmakers
    Animal Handlers
-> Criminals (someone who starts as e.g. a farmer and turns to crime has been reduced to a hinin and will be recognized as such if caught)
-> Bankruptees (the particulars vary by the laws of different localities, but e.g. merchants can be reduced to mere laborers through bankruptcy)

Eta (the "untouchables" of the hinin class, such that all eta are hinin but not all hinin are eta):
-> Tanners
-> Fullers
-> Butchers
-> Cremators
-> Morticians (this is often work performed by monks, especially in cities)
-> "Poopsmiths" (the actual term is "Owaiya" which refers to people who handle "night soil")
-> Torturers (extremely rare, but occasionally employed under certain legally restricted circumstances)
-> Executioners (samurai executions are performed by samurai, but all other executions are performed by eta)

Heimen:
-> Farmers
-> Merchants (the line between "merchant" and one of the below categories is often meaningless)
-> Carpenters
-> Masons
-> Smiths
-> Thatchers
-> Coopers
-> Potters
-> Joiners
-> Weavers (and other fibercrafters of all sorts, spinners, embroiderers, sewers, tailors, etc)
-> Mirror polishers
-> Togishi (sword polishers)
-> Jewelers
-> Painters
-> Sculptors
-> Scribes
-> Leather workers (note that this is different from Eta tanners)
-> Oil pressers
-> Soy sauce brewers
-> Sake brewers
-> Millers
-> Noodlers
-> Sesame grinders
-> Confectioners
-> Tofuya
-> Millwrights
