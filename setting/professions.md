# Ashigaru, Monks, Entertainers, and Bandits

ASHIGARU:

One male from every family of farmers with at least two males in the countryside recieves martial training at least once per month.

This is about 10% of farmers, which means about 8% of the countryside have martial training.  The ashigaru deemed good enough (at least 200 XP, which is roughly 20% of the population) are allowed to carry peasant weapons.  So about 2% of farmers are armed and capable of helping deal with criminals and respond to violence with the legal use of weapons.

In a village with its surrounding hamlets (where there are no samurai), there are about 80 ashigaru, of which about 15-20 are good enough to be armed.  We refer to these as "licensed" ashigaru, and those who have not demonstrated enough proficiency to be permitted to carry weapons unsupervised are known as "unlicensed ashigaru".

MONKS:

Shrines and monasteries are controlled by the Ministry of Rites, and are organized as follows:
- each village district has a Country Monk
- each town has a Preceptor per Order
- each province has an Abbot per Order
- each capitol city has a Grand Abbot per Order

Monk ranks are discussed elsewhere, but shrines and temples are staffed by many monks who report to the leaders mentioned above, and their ranks are generally ordered like this:
- Initiate
- Novice
- Acolyte
- Junior Monk
- Adept Monk (Country Monk is equivalent to this)
- Senior Monk (Town Preceptor is equivalent to this)
- Steward
- Abbot
- Grand Abbot

ENTERTAINERS:

Each county generally has an acting troupe, which usually consists of around 5-10 entertainers.  Acting troupes usually contains not only actors but also other entertainers such as jugglers and musicians, who might perform acts before/during/after plays and skits or be incorporated into larger performances.

Approximately once per week, the town in the center of the county will have a "market day", when farmers throughout the county come to town to buy, sell, and barter.  Acting troupes usually make most of their money on market day, though they will frequently travel through the village districts of the county during the rest of the week to perform.

Such "country troupes" perform mainly for peasants, though they must also be prepared for samurai in the audience on market day, and also be capable of giving special performances at the County Magistrates manor for the magistrate and their samurai retainers.

Provincial cities are large enough to usually have several acting troupes in competition with each other, and a domain capital will have numerous entertainers, some devoted to a particular establishment and others working as solo street performers.

Like monks, entertainers are small part of the population (under 1%) but have an outsized influence on the culture and on the daily lives of those they interact with.

BANDITS:

The average domain has about 200 bandits, most of which have a day job but also commit acts of banditry on the side  These acts include:
 - stealing food or other supplies when no one is around
 - mugging or otherwise shaking down peasants where they live/work
 - acting as a highwayman, robbing people on the road
 - joining with other bandits as a gang to attack caravans

Only bandits who are reasonably strong (over 200 XP) will try anything more bold than stealing and hoping no one sees them.  This means that there are about 45 bandits per domain that will use or threaten violence.  Of these, about 10 will be good enough (over 250 XP, which is about 10% of the population) to act as highwaymen or lead bandit gangs.

So the average domain has about 1-5 groups of bandits attacking wealthy travellers/caravans.  These groups usually consist of 4-8 bandits, although they occasionally grow much larger.

About 10 violent bandits are killed each year per domain, both from casualties during their attacks and being caught by the authorities.

MAHO-TSUKAI:

"Maho" is heretical blood magic, and a "tsukai" is a witch who performs it.  A "bloodspeaker" is a type of tsukai who has learned to conceal the Shadowlands taint which infects the bodies of maho practitioners.

Maho is powered by the "strength" of the names of their victims.  Names can be given freely as part of some dark bargain with those desperate enough to turn to a tsukai for help, or used by the tsukai at the moment of death when murdering a victim.  The more famous a person, the more "strong" and "powerful" their name, and thus tsukai rarely target members of the lower classes, instead preferring to kill or bargain with the wealthy and powerful in order to make use of their powerful names.

A typical coven consists of 3-10 bloodspeakers, usually poor peasants. It's rare to find bloodspeakers among samurai or wealthy peasants.

Only a small percentage of covens are violent.  These covens are typically led by a powerful bloodspeaker who previously spread his name violently in the countryside after reaching Rank 5 and taking on the Jama surname.  Thus, about one out of every three domains has an active coven kidnapping and murdering well-known merchants and nobles.

There are about 200 Witch Hunters.  Each Witch Hunter covers about three domains, with large and important cities/domains getting their own Witch Hunter.

It is unknown how many "wild" maho-tsukai exist out in the countryside.  Remember that around 70% of the human population of a domain is in village districts and their surrounding hamlets, where no samurai live - thus, unless the locals report suspicious activity to the authorities the samurai have no way of knowing whether the countryside is free of witches or if instead the farmers are happily making use of their services (or being threatened into silence).
